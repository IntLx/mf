Success Stories

Scientists discovered that a hole was forming in the ozone layer, which protects the Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation. The hole was caused by human-made chemicals called chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs). In 1987, the Montreal Protocol was signed, which phased out the production and use of CFCs. As a result of the Montreal Protocol, the ozone hole is now recovering.
  • In the 1980s, scientists discovered that a hole was forming in the ozone layer, which protects the Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation.
  • The hole was caused by human-made chemicals called chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs).
  • CFCs are used in a variety of products, including refrigerators, air conditioners, and aerosol cans.
  • When CFCs are released into the atmosphere, they break down ozone molecules.
  • Ozone molecules are essential for protecting the Earth from harmful ultraviolet radiation.
  • Ultraviolet radiation can cause skin cancer, cataracts, and other health problems.
  • In 1987, the Montreal Protocol was signed, which phased out the production and use of CFCs.
  • As a result of the Montreal Protocol, the ozone hole is now recovering.
  • The ozone layer is expected to fully recover by the middle of the 21st century.


Acid rain is caused by the emission of sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides into the atmosphere. These pollutants react with water vapor to form acids, which can fall to the Earth as rain, snow, or fog. Acid rain can damage forests, lakes, and other ecosystems. In the 1970s and 1980s, acid rain was a major problem in many parts of the world. However, thanks to government regulations and the use of cleaner technologies, acid rain is now decreasing in many parts of the world.
